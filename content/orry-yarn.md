# Orry Yarn

## What it is
Orry Yarn is The Orry Mill's own British-made yarn brand: a modern Scottish yarn rooted in place, craft, and community. It launched through a Kickstarter campaign (December 2025 – January 2026), which funded successfully, and is now in fulfilment and early growth.

## Positioning
**Modern craft with Scottish soul** — a contemporary, colour-led brand with traceable British production and a strong sense of place.

### Brand pillars
1. **Place** — Scottish landscape, Ayrshire and west-coast heritage.
2. **Design** — contemporary, colour-led, minimal but expressive.
3. **Transparency** — traceable production, British manufacturing.
4. **Community** — Yarn School, knit-alongs, shared learning.
5. **Education** — patterns, kits, and video guidance.

## Colour palette
A core design asset: **16 colours** drawn from the Scottish landscape — granite, heather, ferns, sand, sea light, and moorland weather. The palette is designed to work harmoniously together and to encourage colourwork.

## Founding narrative
Orry Yarn grew from Scottish textile heritage — the Eaglesham cotton-mill history, the "Orry" village green, and the light and weather of the west coast. Thilde grew up knitting in Denmark, studied at Glasgow School of Art, settled in Scotland, and built The Orry Mill as a craft community. The yarn is an attempt to build a modern craft tradition connected to that history.

> Orry Yarn is a British-made yarn brand born in Scotland, inspired by coastal light, moorland landscapes, and modern craft.

## Launch patterns
Fifteen patterns designed by Thilde, named from Ayrshire geography, Scottish heritage, and textile history: The Orry Fern Beanie; Darvel Cabled Beanie; Darvel Cabled Cowl; Eglinton Houndstooth Beanie; Eglinton Houndstooth Cowl; Maybole Mittens; Alloway Raindrops Blanket; Cassilis Scarf; Cassilis Baby Cardigan; Cassilis Baby Blanket; Lochlea Baby Blanket; Weavers Way Linen Stitch Cowl; Polnoon Tank Top; Lintie Vest; Ailsa Jumper.

## Reference points (spirit)
Comparable in ambition to Purl Soho, Knitting for Olive, and Brooklyn Tweed — but rooted in Scottish heritage and landscape.
