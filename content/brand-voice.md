# Brand Voice & Tone

## In one line
Modern craft with Scottish soul — passionate, Glasgow-cool, and rooted in place.

## Who we are (context for writing)
The Orry Mill is an independent yarn and knitting shop in Eaglesham, just outside Glasgow, with a long-standing community of knitters and an online store. Orry Yarn is our own British-made yarn brand. Thilde — an expert knitter and pattern designer trained at Glasgow School of Art — leads the creative and the voice. We are a small, founder-run business standing deliberately against corporate blandness.

## Voice principles
- **Warm, not saccharine.** We talk to makers like friends in the shop, not customers in a funnel.
- **Confident and knowledgeable.** Years of teaching and knitting sit behind every word. We explain without condescending.
- **Rooted in place.** Scotland, Ayrshire, the west coast — coastal light and moorland weather are part of who we are. Use them honestly, never as cliché.
- **Modern and considered.** Contemporary, colour-led, minimal but expressive. Design-literate.
- **Generous.** We lower the barrier to making. Encouraging to beginners, never gatekeeping.

## Tone by context
- **Product copy:** evocative but precise. Lead with feeling and use, then the practical detail a knitter needs (fibre, weight, metreage, needle size).
- **Education / Yarn School:** patient, step-by-step, reassuring. Assume curiosity, not prior expertise.
- **Community / social:** personable, behind-the-scenes, first person. Thilde's voice.
- **Service / policy:** plain, clear, calm. No jargon, no defensiveness.

## Do
- Write in British English.
- Use specific, sensory colour and landscape language ("granite", "heather", "sea light").
- Name the place and the maker.
- Keep sentences readable; vary their length.

## Don't
- Don't lean on tartan-and-shortbread cliché or "wee" stereotyping.
- Don't overpromise or use hard-sell urgency tactics.
- Don't bury the practical detail a knitter needs to buy with confidence.
- Don't sound like a faceless retailer.

## Reference points (spirit, not imitation)
We admire Purl Soho, Knitting for Olive, and Brooklyn Tweed — premium, design-led craft brands — but our story is Scottish heritage and landscape, which is ours alone.
